States of Matter

What is Matter?

Everything around us is made of matter. Matter is anything that has mass and takes up space. Matter can exist in three main states: solid, liquid, and gas.

Examples: Ice (solid), water (liquid), steam (gas) - all are the same substance (water) in different states!

üßä

Solids

Characteristics: Have definite shape, have definite volume, particles are packed tightly together
Examples: Rock, ice, wood, metal, book

üíß

Liquids

Characteristics: Take shape of container, have definite volume, particles can slide past each other
Examples: Water, milk, juice, oil, honey

üí®

Gases

Characteristics: No definite shape, no definite volume, particles move freely and spread out
Examples: Air, oxygen, helium, steam, carbon dioxide

Changing States of Matter

Matter can change from one state to another when we add or remove heat. These changes are called physical changes.

Melting: Solid ‚Üí Liquid (adding heat)
Freezing: Liquid ‚Üí Solid (removing heat)
Evaporation: Liquid ‚Üí Gas (adding heat)
Condensation: Gas ‚Üí Liquid (removing heat)

Weather & Seasons

What is Weather?

Weather is what the air and atmosphere are like each day. It includes temperature, precipitation (rain, snow), wind, and cloud cover.

üå∑

Spring

Months: March, April, May
Weather: Warming temperatures, rain showers
What happens: Plants grow, flowers bloom, animals have babies

‚òÄÔ∏è

Summer

Months: June, July, August
Weather: Hot temperatures, thunderstorms
What happens: Long days, plants are fully grown, fruits ripen

üçÇ

Fall (Autumn)

Months: September, October, November
Weather: Cooling temperatures, windy
What happens: Leaves change color and fall, animals prepare for winter

‚õÑ

Winter

Months: December, January, February
Weather: Cold temperatures, snow, ice
What happens: Short days, some animals hibernate, plants rest

Types of Weather

Weather can be described in many ways. Meteorologists are scientists who study and predict the weather.

Sunny: Bright with lots of sunshine
Cloudy: Sky covered with clouds
Rainy: Water falling from clouds
Snowy: Frozen water falling from clouds
Windy: Air moving quickly
Stormy: Thunder, lightning, heavy rain or snow

Materials & Their Uses

What Are Materials?

Materials are the substances that things are made from. Different materials have different properties that make them useful for specific purposes.

ü™µ

Wood

Properties: Strong, can be cut and shaped, floats
Uses: Furniture, houses, paper, pencils

üî©

Metal

Properties: Strong, hard, conducts heat and electricity
Uses: Cars, buildings, cans, wires, tools

üß¥

Plastic

Properties: Lightweight, waterproof, can be molded
Uses: Bottles, toys, containers, bags

ü•õ

Glass

Properties: Transparent, hard, breaks easily
Uses: Windows, bottles, glasses, light bulbs

üëï

Fabric

Properties: Flexible, soft, can be woven
Uses: Clothing, blankets, curtains, towels

üõû

Rubber

Properties: Flexible, waterproof, bouncy
Uses: Tires, erasers, boots, balls

Material Properties

We choose materials based on their properties - the special qualities they have.

Strong: Can hold heavy things without breaking
Flexible: Can bend without breaking
Waterproof: Does not let water through
Transparent: You can see through it
Conductor: Allows heat or electricity to pass through
Insulator: Does not allow heat or electricity to pass through
