Pronouns Practice

What are Pronouns?

Pronouns are words that take the place of nouns. They help us avoid repeating the same nouns over and over.

Without pronouns: "Sarah said Sarah would bring Sarah's book to Sarah's friend."

With pronouns: "Sarah said she would bring her book to her friend."

Common Pronouns:

  • Subject Pronouns: I, you, he, she, it, we, they
  • Object Pronouns: me, you, him, her, it, us, them
  • Possessive Pronouns: my, your, his, her, its, our, their

Prepositions Practice

What are Prepositions?

Prepositions are words that show the relationship between a noun or pronoun and other words in a sentence. They often tell us about position, time, or direction.

Position: The cat is under the table.

Time: We will meet at 3 o'clock.

Direction: She walked toward the school.

Common Prepositions:

  • Position: in, on, under, above, below, between, beside
  • Time: at, on, in, before, after, during
  • Direction: to, from, into, out of, toward, through

Conjunctions Practice

What are Conjunctions?

Conjunctions are words that connect words, phrases, or sentences together. They help us combine ideas and make our writing flow better.

Without conjunction: "I like apples. I like oranges."

With conjunction: "I like apples and oranges."

Common Conjunctions:

  • Adding ideas: and, also, plus
  • Showing contrast: but, yet, however
  • Showing choice: or, either...or
  • Showing cause/effect: because, so, therefore

Grammar Rules Summary

Pronouns

Use subject pronouns (I, you, he, she, it, we, they) as the subject of a sentence. Use object pronouns (me, you, him, her, it, us, them) after verbs or prepositions.

Prepositions

Prepositions show relationships. Use 'in' for enclosed spaces, 'on' for surfaces, 'at' for specific times/places. Remember: The book is ON the table, IN the bag, or UNDER the chair.

Conjunctions

Use 'and' to add ideas, 'but' to show contrast, 'or' to show choice, 'because' to give reasons, and 'so' to show results. Always use a comma before conjunctions in compound sentences.
